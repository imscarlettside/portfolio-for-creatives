Múgyuè Music Festival

Branding /// Iconography /// Wayfinding

Research shows that the language demographics of Vancouver are extremely diverse, with 42.9% of Metro Vancouver residents having a non-English language as their first language. With such a large 1st generation immigrant population in Vancouver, how can we foster community across the boundary of language?

Introducing Múgyuè

Múgyuè (moo-gway) is a three-day Vancouver music festival celebrating the city's cultural tapestry. Each day is dedicated to a different language—Spanish, Punjabi, or Mandarin—and features local artists who perform in that language. Held in neighbourhoods less familiar with the featured language, the festival encourages all Vancouverites to explore new sounds and share their cultures.

Posters

A poster series uses bold colour and typography to convey celebration and connection. Each poster highlights a headlining artist's name in its native script, using the accessible Noto typeface family. 

Wayfinding & Lanyard

Secondary elements, like text on lanyards, have two calls to action; one in English encouraging exploration, and another in the featured languages inviting cultural sharing.
