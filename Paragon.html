Paragon Workwear

Art direction /// Poster Design /// Social Media

Paragon is an all-gender workwear brand that battles two problems: the lack of inclusive workwear for women in trades and the industry's massive textile waste.

I led the creation of the brand's visual identity and all collateral presented on this page. This was a collaborative project with Alexis Chiang, who focused on the website. See her work here.

Sustainable Material

All Paragon products are made from Piñatex, a durable leather alternative woven from pineapple leaves that would otherwise be discarded. Inspired by the microscopic cross section of the pineapple leaf, our brand graphics represents the strength of both our material and the resilient workers who wear it.

Tone & photography

Paragon's tone frames tradespeople as the unsung heroes of our society, celebrating their strength and tenacity.  This is visually reinforced by photography that normalizes women on the job site, directly challenging the exclusion and mistreatment they experience by portraying them as capable, authoritative, and belonging.
