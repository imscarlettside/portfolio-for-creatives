FEA: Foundation of Expressive Art

Branding /// Packaging /// Motion

Many people could benefit from expressive art therapy, a flexible creative approach to healing, but do not access it due to misconceptions that it requires artistic skill or due to practical barriers like cost and scheduling. To solve this, I developed Fea, a foundation that reframes the practice as accessible and grounded in judgment free play. The visual language supports this with minimalist design and crayon strokes to balance professionalism with approachability. Clear, empathetic messaging demystifies the therapy and guides people toward resources that fit their lives.

The kit

This kit provides a self-paced entry point to expressive art therapy for those who may not have the time to meet with a therapist every week. Its supplies, such as the crayons and egg shakers, reference childhood which encourages a childlike approach to creativity.

The cards

Created with the help of a professional therapist, these cards structure the expressive process into approachable steps, making the therapeutic methodology accessible for independent use.

Website

The website uses clean layouts and clear resource filters to create a trustworthy hub for expressive art therapy. It directly connects to flexible, affordable therapy options, guaranteeing that those in need will find an option that will work for them.

Posters

Dynamic photography overlaid with a single, continuous crayon line visually demonstrates how different creative forms are woven into one integrated therapeutic process.

Social media grid

This social media grid uses a consistent visual language of minimalist design and crayon strokes which builds recognition while sharing educational content and therapist highlights to spread awareness.
